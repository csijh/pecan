Transliteration

The first stage is to extract the rules from Appendix A of the C11 standard, and carry out a few obvious cosmetic changes for compatibility with Pecan. The result is presented in this file, which acts as a starting point for transformations.

c11-grammar.txt

Terminal symbols in bold such as int are quoted as "int" instead. Definitions x: y have the colon replaced by an equal sign to give x = y, the optional suffix xopt is replaced by a question mark x?, and choices which are implicit from the layout have explicit choice operators | added. Rules are effectively presented in a mythical language which is Pecan with an extra | operator having CFG semantics. The aim is to apply transformations until the alternatives of any | operator can clearly be distinguished by the PEG operator / instead, so that the | operator can be eliminated.

The transformations of the rules will be described in the order of their appearance in appendix A of the C11 standard, and the result will be presented as a separate scanner and parser. During transformations, rules taken from the standard, or needing more transformation work, are displayed in blue, whereas final Pecan rules are displayed in green. In describing transformations, only the first example of each straightforward technique, plus awkward issues, will be described.
