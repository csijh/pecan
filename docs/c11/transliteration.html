Transliteration

The grammar rules from Appendix A of the C11 standard have been extracted, and a few obvious cosmetic changes carried out for compatibility with Pecan. The result is presented in this file, which acts as a starting point for transformations.

c11-grammar.txt

Terminal symbols in bold such as int are quoted as "int" instead. Definitions x: y have the colon replaced by an equal sign to give x = y, the optional suffix xopt is replaced by a question mark x?, and choices which are implicit from the layout have explicit choice operators | added. Rules are effectively presented in a mythical language which is Pecan with an extra | operator having CFG semantics. The aim will be to apply transformations until the alternatives of any CFG choice operator | can clearly be distinguished by Pecan's choice operator / instead, so that the | operator can be eliminated.

During transformations of the grammar, rules taken from the above file, or needing more transformation work, are displayed in blue, whereas final Pecan rules are displayed in green.
